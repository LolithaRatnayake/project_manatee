### 2. Key Lessons & Mindset Takeaways

- **Boundary Definition & Scope Control:** An architect must first ensure stability and clarity within their immediate domain ("Is my house clean?") before managing external integrations. You cannot effectively architect touchpoints with external systems until your internal domain boundaries are rigidly defined and managed.
- **The "Ivory Tower" as a North Star:** While the term "Ivory Tower" often carries a negative connotation of being disconnected, in this context, it represents the idealized **Target Architecture (Future State)**. Your job is to paint this high-level, aspirational vision of where the company or department needs to go.
- **Value-Driven Architecture:** Technology choices do not exist in a vacuum. Architecture decisions ultimately influence sales and business viability, meaning every architectural trade-off must be calculated using a **cost-to-value delivered** metric.

### 3. Mentor Feedback Summary
