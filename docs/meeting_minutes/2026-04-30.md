## ## Week-at-a-Glance: April 30, 2026

The focus of this session was defining the operational mechanics and user personas for a Large Language Model (LLM) powered Proof of Concept (PoC) designed to detect **architecture drift**. The discussion pivoted away from real-time remediation toward robust state tracking and governance workflow.

---

## ## Key Lessons

- **State Retention over Immediate Remediation:** In enterprise governance, capturing and logging a variance (drift) is structurally more important than immediately suggesting a fix. Storage allows for trend analysis and historical auditing.
- **Idempotency and Lifecycle Tracking:** A governance tool must not evaluate compliance in a vacuum. It needs to look backward to verify if previously identified drift has been remediated or accepted before flagging new issues.
- **Separation of Concerns via Personas:** Architecture governance workflows must distinctly separate the strategic overview (Architect Persona) from the tactical execution (Implementation Team Persona).

---

## ## Mentor Feedback Summary

Your mentor is guiding you toward building a **governance framework**, not just a technical script. The feedback emphasizes structured workflow steps:

1. **Detection:** Identify the delta between the target architecture and the actual implementation.
2. **Persistence:** Store the drift data immediately rather than rushing to a recommendation engine.
3. **Role-Based Execution:** Tailor the output to the specific needs of the Architect vs. the Implementer.
4. **Closed-Loop Verification:** Ensure subsequent runs execute a delta-check against historical state data to close the loop on outstanding drift.
